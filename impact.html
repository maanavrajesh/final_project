TRIPPY

Social theory and impact

Trippy applies trust and social capital theory to mobility coordination.

People are more willing to coordinate travel when they can assess familiarity, group identity, and the reliability of the people around them.

Social theory

Trust reduces uncertainty

Visible identity signals such as school affiliation, year, Instagram presence, and a short self-description help users evaluate whether a match feels socially legitimate.

Organizational impact

Safer and more coordinated campus mobility

Campuses benefit when students can leave events and study spaces more efficiently, with lower reliance on ad hoc communication and fewer solo travel decisions.

Societal impact

More connected and accessible movement

Trippy can support safer routines, broader social connection, and more flexible transport choices for students who may not always want or afford a solo ride.

Why this matters

Better information changes behavior

If students can quickly see that someone nearby shares their route, timing, and social comfort level, the decision to travel together becomes much easier.

That makes Trippy more than a convenience app. It becomes a system that restructures how people perceive safety, coordination, and trust in everyday movement.

Trippy’s expected impact is grounded in safer coordination, stronger peer networks, and more usable travel choices.
